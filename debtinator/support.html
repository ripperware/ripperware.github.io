FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How does Debtinator calculate my payoff date?

Debtinator uses the Debt Avalanche method — minimum payments on all debts, with every extra dollar attacking the highest interest rate debt first. Once a debt is eliminated, its payment is redirected to the next target. The engine runs month-by-month simulations to calculate your exact payoff date and total interest paid.

Why does my credit card minimum payment change each month?

Credit card minimums are dynamic — most issuers charge approximately 2% of your current balance or $25, whichever is higher. As your balance drops, your minimum drops too. Debtinator recalculates this each month for accuracy.

What is a statement closing date and why does it matter?

Your statement closing date is the day your billing cycle ends. Interest is calculated on your balance on that date. If you pay before your statement closes, interest is charged on your reduced balance — saving you money. Entering this date in Debtinator improves calculation accuracy for credit cards.

Is my financial data safe?

Yes. All data you enter is stored locally on your device only. Nothing is transmitted to Ripperware or any third-party server. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

How do I delete all my data?

Go to Missions & Settings → scroll to the Settings section → tap Reset All Data. A 5-second countdown will appear. You can cancel during the countdown. Once confirmed, all missions and payment logs are permanently deleted.

Can I have multiple debt elimination missions?

Yes — Debtinator supports unlimited missions. Tap the + NEW MISSION button in Missions & Settings to create additional missions. All saved missions are accessible from the Missions & Settings screen.

What if I want to change my income or debts after starting a mission?

Tap the Edit Mission button on your results screen. You can update your income, expenses, and debt details. Your payment logs will be preserved and your payment schedule will be recalculated based on the new parameters.

Does Debtinator support mortgages or HELOCs?

Not currently. Debtinator is optimized for credit cards, auto loans, personal loans, and student loans. Mortgage and HELOC support is planned for a future update.

The calculations seem different from my actual statements. Why?

Debtinator uses standard financial formulas as estimates. Actual results may vary based on your specific loan terms, issuer policies, fees, and daily compounding differences. For best accuracy, use your live account balance (not your statement balance), and enter your due date and statement closing date for credit cards.

How do I view the onboarding guide again?

Go to Missions & Settings → scroll to the Settings section → tap View Onboarding.
